Allison Bennett's 7 Forms of Respect™ Results

How You Give Respect

The 7 Forms of Respect™ Defined:

Attention: Being present; not multitasking; listening attentively

Candor: Asking challenging questions; giving unsolicited, constructive feedback

Consideration: Letting what you think people need guide your interactions with them

Procedure: Following the established process; asking for and setting clear expectations

Acknowledgment: Expressing gratitude and acknowledgment of work done; praise

Punctuality: Being punctual; staying on schedule; meeting deadlines

Information: Providing access to information that requires no action

Your top form of getting respect is CANDOR

Generally, those who value giving candor in the workplace provide constructive criticism and want to be push those they work with to be the best that they can be. This may mean giving feedback even when work is still in progress, asking challenging questions, or helping out coworker when they are stuck.
